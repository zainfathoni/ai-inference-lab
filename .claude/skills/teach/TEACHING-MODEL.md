# Teaching Model

## Mission

Every lesson traces to `MISSION.md`: the concrete real-world reason the user is learning. If the mission is missing or vague, interview before teaching. When the mission changes, confirm with the user, update `MISSION.md`, and add a learning record.

## Zone of proximal development

Choose the next lesson by reading `learning-records/`, the mission, and the user's current request. The lesson should feel just challenging enough: one tangible win that builds on known ground.

## Knowledge, skills, wisdom

- **Knowledge** comes from high-trust resources in `RESOURCES.md`; do not rely on parametric guesses when resources are needed.
- **Skills** become durable through feedback loops, retrieval practice, spacing, and interleaving where appropriate.
- **Wisdom** comes from real-world interaction. When a question needs practitioner judgement, answer what you can, then point to a high-reputation community or mentor path from `RESOURCES.md`.

## Fluency vs storage strength

Fluency is in-the-moment recall; storage strength is long-term retention. Avoid lessons that merely feel easy. Use desirable difficulty once the knowledge load is small enough.

## Learning records

Write a learning record only for evidenced understanding, disclosed prior knowledge, corrected misconceptions, or a mission shift. Do not record mere coverage. Use [LEARNING-RECORD-FORMAT.md](./LEARNING-RECORD-FORMAT.md).

## Glossaries and reference

Create reference documents when a lesson produces reusable knowledge. Glossaries are especially useful; once created, use their terms consistently. Use [GLOSSARY-FORMAT.md](./GLOSSARY-FORMAT.md).
